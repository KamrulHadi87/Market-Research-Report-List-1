<p><p>Sodium Carboxy-Methylcellulose (CMC) is a versatile cellulose derivative used in various industries. The Industrial Grade CMC is primarily utilized in applications like paper, textiles, and pharmaceutical manufacturing, where it serves as a thickening, stabilizing, and binding agent. In contrast, Food Grade CMC is specifically formulated for use in food products, acting as a thickener, emulsifier, or fat replacer, ensuring texture and stability in items like sauces, ice creams, and baked goods. Both grades cater to distinct regulatory and safety standards based on their applications.</p></p>
