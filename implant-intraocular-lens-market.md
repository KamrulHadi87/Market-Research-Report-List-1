<p><p>The Implant Intraocular Lens (IOL) market is divided into two main types: non-foldable lenses and foldable lenses. Non-foldable lenses are rigid and typically require larger incisions during cataract surgery, making them less common in modern practice. In contrast, foldable intraocular lenses are made from flexible materials, allowing them to be inserted through smaller incisions, promoting quicker recovery and minimal complications. The foldable IOL segment has gained popularity due to these advantages, catering to the growing demand for minimally invasive surgical solutions.</p></p>
