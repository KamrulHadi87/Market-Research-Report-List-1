<p><p>The Uterine Fibroid Embolization Agents Market includes various types of embolic agents used to block blood flow to fibroids. Gelatin sponges are resorbable, providing temporary occlusion. Polyvinyl Alcohol (PVA) particles are permanent agents that cause fibrosis. Trisacryl Gelatin Microspheres (TAGM) are biocompatible and offer controlled particle sizes for effective embolization. Polymethyl Methacrylate (PMMA) microspheres create a permanent blockage but may pose risks of complications. Other materials may also be used depending on clinical needs and patient considerations.</p></p>
