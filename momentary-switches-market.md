<p><ul><li>Push-to-Break Momentary Switches</li><li>Push-to-Make Momentary Switches</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Momentary switches are electrical devices that only maintain a connection while they are being pressed. The market features two primary types: Push-to-Break momentary switches interrupt the current flow when pressed, typically used for emergency stops or safety applications. Conversely, Push-to-Make momentary switches establish a connection while activated, commonly found in start buttons and triggers. Both switch types are crucial in various industries, including automotive, electronics, and industrial controls, ensuring reliable operation in diverse applications.</p></p>
