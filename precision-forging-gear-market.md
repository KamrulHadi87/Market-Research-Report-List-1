<p><p>Precision forging in the gear market includes cold forging and warm forging processes. Cold forging involves shaping metal at room temperature, resulting in high dimensional accuracy and improved mechanical properties, making it suitable for intricate designs. Warm forging, on the other hand, occurs at elevated temperatures, allowing for easier shaping while maintaining better material ductility. Both processes are essential for producing durable, high-performance gears tailored for various applications in industries like automotive, aerospace, and machinery manufacturing.</p></p>
