<p><p>Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography (EUVL) is an advanced photolithography technique used in semiconductor manufacturing to produce smaller, more efficient microchips. The EUVL market comprises several segments, including light sources, mirrors, and masks. Light sources generate the extreme ultraviolet light required for the lithography process. Mirrors, specially designed for EUV wavelengths, direct and focus this light onto the wafer. Masks, which contain the circuit patterns, are essential for transferring designs onto silicon wafers, ensuring precision in the chip fabrication process.</p></p>
