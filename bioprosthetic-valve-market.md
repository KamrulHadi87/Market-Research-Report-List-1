<p><p>A bioprosthetic valve is a type of heart valve replacement made from biological tissues, typically harvested from animal sources like pigs or cows. These valves are designed to mimic natural heart valve function, providing a viable alternative for patients with valvular heart diseases. <p><ul><li>Tissue Aortic Valve</li><li>Tissue Mitral Valve</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bioprosthetic valve market primarily encompasses two types: tissue aortic valves and tissue mitral valves. Tissue aortic valves are designed to replace damaged aortic valves, usually made from animal tissues, offering enhanced biocompatibility and reduced risk of thrombosis. Tissue mitral valves, similarly crafted from biological materials, address mitral valve diseases and dysfunctions. Both types are favored for their natural feel and performance, providing patients with effective treatment options while minimizing the need for long-term anticoagulation therapy.</p></p>
